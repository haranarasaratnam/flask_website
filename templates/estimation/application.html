SLAM: Simultaneous Localization & Map Building

Application

  • We cosnider a localization problem where a car-like robot employs the SCKF to eatimate its own pose based on the landmarks and on-board sensor measurements.
  • The robot’s pose (state vector), represented as \((x, y, \theta)\), is initialized using a less precise map dataset, akin to GPS accuracy.
  • As the robot moves, it continuously adjusts its velocity and steering angle.
  • An onboard radar sensor measures the range and bearing with respect to known landmarks, provided they are within the sensor’s detection range and field of view.
  • In addition to achieving sufficient accuracy to localize the robot within a confined area, this demands an efficient estimation algorithm to meet strict time constraints, ensuring the process operated within the allowable runtime limits without failure.

Dynamic State-Space Model

  • Process Eqn: \[ \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,k}\\ x_{2,k}\\ x_{3,k} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,k-1} + u_{1,k}\cos(x_{3,k-1}) - u_{2,k}\sin(x_{3,k-1})\\ x_{2,k-1} + u_{1,k}\sin(x_{3,k-1}) + u_{2,k}\cos(x_{3,k-1})\\ x_{3,k-1} + u_{3,k} \end{bmatrix} + \mathbf{v}_{k} \]
  • Measurement Eqn: \[ \begin{bmatrix} z_{1,k}\\ z_{2,k} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{(x_{1,k} - x_{\text{lmk},i})^{2}+(x_{2,k} - y_{\text{lmk},i})^{2}}\\ \tan^{-1}(\frac{(x_{2,k} - y_{\text{lmk},i})}{(x_{1,k} - x_{\text{lmk},i})}) - x_{3,k} \end{bmatrix} + \mathbf{w}_{k} \]

Experimental Settings

  • The initial position of the robot was set to \([40m, 25m, 0^\circ]\) and the trajectory was simulated for 120 seconds.
  • The radar was assumed to have a limited line of sight, detecting only landmarks within a range of 50m and an angular field of view of 30 degrees.
  • The control input was set to be constant at \([0, 1, 3^\circ]\) with added Gaussian noise characterized by a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of \([0.01m, 0.01m, 1.5^\circ]\).
  • The on-board radar measurements were modeled as noisy, with Gaussian-distributed noise having a mean of 0 and a standard deviation of \([1.1m, 5^\circ]\).
  • We randomly placed 15 landmarks in the environment. Radar measurements in the local coordinate frame were transformed into the global coordinate system in the x and y planes. The nearest neighbor technique was then applied to identify the corresponding landmarks.
