Square-Root Cubature Kalman Filters (SCKF)

Intro

  • In order to preserve the covarinace matrix properties such as symmetry and positive simi-definiteness, a square-root formulation is required
  • A covariance matrix of \(\mathbf{P}\in R^{n\times n}\)can be factorized using a traingulrization procedure (e.g. QR decomposition) as follows: \[ \mathbf{P} = \mathbf{S}\mathbf{S}^{T} \] where \(S\in R^{n\times n}\) is an upper triangular (sparse) matrix.
  • We may therefore write the predicted state error covariance in a straightforward way.
  • For the measurement update however, we rewrite the following covarinces in the form of squared-matrices: \[ \begin{align} \mathbf{P}_{k|k-1} &= X_{k|k-1}X^{T}_{k|k-1}\\ \mathbf{P}_{zz,k|k-1} &= Z_{k|k-1}Z^{T}_{k|k-1} + \mathbf{S}_{R, k+1}\mathbf{S}^{T}_{R, k+1}\\ \mathbf{P}_{xz, k|k-1} &= X_{k|k-1}Z^{T}_{k|k-1} \end{align} \]

Intro

  • We combine the above 3 in a square-matrix form as shown by \[ \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{P}_{zz, k|k-1} & \mathbf{P}_{zx, k|k-1}\\ \mathbf{P}_{xz, k|k-1} & \mathbf{P}_{k|k-1} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} Z_{k|k-1} & \mathbf{S}_{R,k}\\ X_{k|k-1} & \mathbf{O} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} Z_{k|k-1} & \mathbf{S}_{R,k}\\ X_{k|k-1} & \mathbf{O} \end{pmatrix}^{T} \] where \(\mathbf{O}\in R^{n\times d}\) is the zero matrix.
  • Applying the triangularization procedure to the square-root factor above, we Gaussian-weighted \[ \text{Tria}\begin{pmatrix} Z_{k|k-1} & \mathbf{S}_{R,k}\\ X_{k|k-1} & \mathbf{O} \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf{T}_{11} & \mathbf{O}\\ \mathbf{T}_{21} & \mathbf{T}_{22} \end{pmatrix} \] where \(\mathbf{T}_{11}\in R^{d\times d}\) and \(\mathbf{T}_{22} \in R^{n\times n}\) are low-triangular matrices and \(\mathbf{T}_{21} \in R^{n \times d}\).
  • Hence, we write \[ \begin{align} \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{P}_{zz, k|k-1} & \mathbf{P}_{zx, k|k-1}\\ \mathbf{P}_{xz, k|k-1} & \mathbf{P}_{k|k-1} \end{bmatrix} &= \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf{T}_{11} & \mathbf{O}\\ \mathbf{T}_{21} & \mathbf{T}_{22} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} \mathbf{T}_{11} & \mathbf{O}\\ \mathbf{T}_{21} & \mathbf{T}_{22} \end{pmatrix}^{T}\\ &= \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{T}_{11}\mathbf{T}_{11}^{T} & \mathbf{T}_{11}\mathbf{T}_{21}^{T}\\ \mathbf{T}_{21}\mathbf{T}_{11}^{T} & \mathbf{T}_{21}\mathbf{T}_{21}^{T}+\mathbf{T}_{22}\mathbf{T}_{22}^{T} \end{bmatrix} \end{align} \]
  • The cubature Kalman gian can be therefore written as \[ \begin{align} W_{k} &= \mathbf{P}_{xz, k|k-1}\mathbf{P}^{-1}_{zz, k|k-1}\\ &=\mathbf{T}_{21}\mathbf{T}_{11}^{T}(\mathbf{T}_{21}\mathbf{T}_{11}^{T})^{-1}\\ &=\mathbf{T}_{21}\mathbf{T}_{11}^{-1} \end{align} \] Since \(\mathbf{T}_{11}\) is a lower traingular matrix, we may efficiently compute \(W_{k}\)
  • By the same token, we may write \[ \mathbf{P}_{k|k} = \mathbf{T}_{22}\mathbf{T}_{22}^{T} \]

Time Update

  • Assume at time \(k\) that the posterior density, \[ p(\mathbf{x}_{k-1}|D_{k-1}) = \mathcal{N}(\hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k-1|k-1}, \mathbf{P}_{k-1|k-1}) \] is known. Factorize \[ \mathbf{P}_{k-1|k-1} = S_{k-1|k-1}S_{k-1|k-1}^{T} \]
  • Evaluate the cubature points: (\(i=1, 2,\ldots m, \text{ where } m=2n_{x}\)) \[ X_{i, k-1|k-1} = S_{k-1|k-1}\xi_{i} + \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k-1|k-1} \]
  • Evaluate the propagated cubature points \((i=1,2,\ldots m)\): \[ X^{\ast}_{i, k|k-1} = \mathbf{f}(X_{i,k-1|k-1}, \mathbf{u}_{k-1}) \]
  • Estimate the predicted state \[ \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k|k-1} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}X^{\ast}_{i, k|k-1} \]
  • Estimate the predicted error covariance \[ \mathbf{P}_{k|k-1} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}X^{\ast}_{i, k|k-1}X^{\ast T}_{i, k|k-1} - \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k|k-1}\hat{\mathbf{x}}^{T}_{k|k-1} + \mathbf{Q}_{k-1} \]

Measurement Update

  • Factorize \[ \mathbf{P}_{k|k-1} = S_{k|k-1}S_{k|k-1}^{T} \]
  • Evaluate the cubature points: (\(i=1, 2,\ldots m, \text{ where } m=2n_{x}\)) \[ X_{i, k|k-1} = S_{k|k-1}\xi_{i} + \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k|k-1} \]
  • Evaluate the propagated cubature points \((i=1,2,\ldots m)\): \[ Z_{i, k|k-1} = \mathbf{f}(X_{i,k|k-1}, \mathbf{u}_{k}) \]
  • Estimate the predicted measurement \[ \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{k|k-1} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}Z_{i, k|k-1} \]
  • Estimate the innovations covariance matrix \[ \mathbf{P}_{zz, k|k-1} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}Z_{i, k|k-1}Z^{T}_{i, k|k-1} - \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{k|k-1}\hat{\mathbf{z}}^{T}_{k|k-1} + \mathbf{R}_{k} \]

Measurement Update (Continued)

  • Estimate the cross-covariance matrix \[ \mathbf{P}_{xz, k|k-1} = \frac{1}{m}\sum_{i=1}^{m}X_{i, k|k-1}Z^{T}_{i, k|k-1} - \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k|k-1}\hat{\mathbf{z}}^{T}_{k|k-1} \]
  • Estimate the cubature Kalman Gain \[ W_{k} = \mathbf{P}_{xz, k|k-1}\mathbf{P}^{-1}_{zz, k|k-1} \]
  • Estimate the updated state \[ \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k|k} = \hat{\mathbf{x}}_{k|k-1} - W_{k}(\mathbf{z}_{k} - \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{k|k-1}) \]
  • Estimate the corresponding posterior error covariance matrix \[ \mathbf{P}_{k|k} = \mathbf{P}_{k|k-1} - W_{k}P_{zz,k|k-1}W^{T}_{k} \]
